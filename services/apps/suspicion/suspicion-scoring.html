Suspicion Scoring Reference

This page documents every output value produced by the suspicion analyzer and explains how each value affects SuspicionScore. The goal is to identify patterns consistent with handicap manipulation (sandbagging), especially when performance changes by context (tournament vs casual), posting behavior, and round composition.

How The Score Works

Additive Signals Each suspicious pattern contributes points. Larger and more repeatable anomalies add more.
Context Checks Many rules require both tournament and casual samples so one mode can be compared against the other.
Sample Guard If tournament rounds are fewer than 3, final score is multiplied by 0.65.

Why this helps: sandbagging is rarely visible from one metric alone. Reliable detection comes from converging signals: strategic underperformance, selective posting behavior, and context-dependent scoring gaps.

Golfer Name (FirstName LastName) Display name for each analyzed golfer, derived from the source filename convention. No direct points. Makes suspicious profiles immediately actionable for committee review without cross-referencing IDs.
GolferID Unique golfer identifier. No direct points. Tracking and attribution only.

Date Range Analyzed (RoundsStartDate to RoundsEndDate) Earliest to latest round date used in the analysis window for that golfer. No direct points. Shows whether suspicious signals are persistent over time or concentrated in a short period.
Handicap Index Computed from most recent 20 rounds using WHS-style best-count logic. Used as baseline in multiple tests (beat-rate and tournament-vs-index gap). If actual event scoring often beats this baseline, index may be inflated.

>=2.0 (+15), >=3.0 (+28), >=5.0 (+45).		 Strong tournament outperformance relative to casual rounds is a primary manipulation pattern.
Tournament vs Casual Gap AvgCasualDifferential - AvgTournamentDifferential. Directly drives the large context-gap scoring above. Large positive gap indicates performance “switching on” when stakes matter.

Overall beat-rate (if 12+ rounds): >=35% (+5), >=45% (+10).		 Typical golfers beat index less frequently; unusually high beat-rate can indicate inflated handicap.
Best8 vs Remaining Diff Gap Difference between average of best 8 and remaining rounds from the most recent 20 rounds. >4.5 (+12), >6.0 (+20). Large spread can indicate intentional “bad” rounds mixed with very strong scoring.

Tournament skew left-heavy: <=-0.60 (+4), <=-0.90 (+8).		 Helps detect distribution shape anomalies, not just averages (e.g., clusters of very low tournament differentials).

PatternRate>=0.60, 2+ patterns, rise/drop >=0.80 (+18).

Avg Blowup Holes (overall/tournament/casual) and Blowup Gap Blow-up holes are raw - par >= 3. Gap compares casual vs tournament. Gap: >=0.8 (+10), >=1.5 (+18). Many more casual blow-ups than tournament blow-ups can signal selective underperformance.

Avg Posting Delay Days, Late Post Count, Late Post Rate, Late Post Rate Casual/Tournament, Late Post Rate Gap Measures timeliness and context bias in score posting. “Late” means 7+ days. High late rate: +8 to +15.
High delay average: +4 to +8.
Casual-tournament late-rate gap: +5 to +10.		 Delayed or selective posting patterns are common in index manipulation workflows.
PlayedAt Day-of-Month Distribution (1-31) ASCII histogram shown by month. Each month has a fixed 31-character day row, where each position represents day 1..31, and each row ends with that month’s total in parentheses (example: (8)). Symbol legend: space = 0 rounds, 1..9 = count for that day, + = 10+ rounds on that day. No direct points currently; diagnostic context only. Helps spot temporal posting/play clustering patterns (for example, repeated concentration on specific day numbers) that may support or challenge other suspicious signals.
Suspicion Score Total weighted score from all triggered rules. Higher = more suspicious pattern density/severity. Aggregates weak and strong signals into one ranking number for triage.
Flags Human-readable reasons for every score contribution and sampling caveat. No extra points by itself; reflects score logic outcomes. Provides auditability and explains why a golfer ranked high.
Score interpretation note: this is a risk-screening model, not proof of intentional misconduct. Always validate with round-level evidence, competition history, and local committee review.

Current Rule Summary (Quick Reference)

  • Large tournament-vs-casual differential gaps are the strongest weighted signal.
  • Tournament scoring far better than handicap index and high beat-index frequency materially increase suspicion.
  • Best-8-vs-rest variance, blow-up behavior, and trajectory timing patterns add corroborating evidence.
  • The PlayedAt histogram is a visual diagnostic for day-of-month clustering; it does not add score points by itself.
  • Posting-delay behavior is a secondary signal that strengthens pattern confidence.
  • Low tournament sample size reduces final score to avoid overconfidence on small data.
